The right of return is a right, grounded in international law and in the moral claim of people who were displaced from their homes during the Nakba. The SHP takes it as such: not a symbolic gesture, not a negotiating chip, and not something to be set aside in the name of pragmatism, or for a “later stage” as suggested in the Oslo accord, but a right whose realisation is essential to any just future for Palestine-Israel. Under the SHP, Palestinian refugees and their descendants become citizens of the State of Palestine and may choose to become permanent residents of the State of Israel. They would enjoy rights in their place of residence inside Israel: schooling for their children, social services, recognition of their language and history, and participation in municipal elections as voters and candidates. They do not vote in Knesset elections; that level of political participation follows citizenship. This decoupling of citizenship from residency is what makes return possible while preserving meaningful national self-determination for both peoples.
