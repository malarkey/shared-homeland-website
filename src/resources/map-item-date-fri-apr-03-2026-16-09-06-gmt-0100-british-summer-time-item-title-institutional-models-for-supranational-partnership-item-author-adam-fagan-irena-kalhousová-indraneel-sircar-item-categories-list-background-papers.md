  His research focuses on civil society, democratic backsliding, and
  re-democratisation in Central and Eastern Europe and the Western Balkans, with
  a current empirical focus on the politics of energy transition in Poland.

She specialises in the Arab-Israeli conflict, security in the Middle East, and relations between Central European countries and Israel, and holds a PhD from the London School of Economics, an MPhil from the University of Cambridge, and a BA from the Hebrew University in Jerusalem. 


  Indraneel Sircar is Associate Professor (Teaching) in Quantitative Research Methods and Political Science at University College London UCL Blogs. His research spans comparative politics, political behaviour, social movements, Southeast Europe, and Europeanization.

item-summary: What lessons can we learn for a future Israel Palestine
  confederation from three distinct models of supranational partnership – the
  EU, ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) and the (Irish) NSMC
  (North-South Ministerial Council) – adopted by sovereign states to address
  matters of common concern? With a particular focus on institutions and
  arrangements in the fields of security, economy, and environmental protection,
  our intention is to provide an overview of a spectrum of approaches involving
  degrees of joint decision-making and differing approaches to dispute
  resolution.
