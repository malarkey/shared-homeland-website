item-summary: This essay asks whether and how ecological conditions can form the
  basis for cooperation across social, spatial, and political borders and
  boundaries. Using case study materials from various parts of the world, and
  with a particular focus on water, this paper seeks to identify the social,
  political, economic, and ecological conditions under which cooperative water
  practices might strengthen collective partnerships or shared habitability
  goals across political boundaries.
